Announces Development on 11 New Comedies For 2018Danielle TurchianoAssociate Features Editor@danielletbd Share This ArticleLinkedInEmailPrintTalkCourtesy of IFCJuly 29, 2017 | 02:30PM PTIFC has announced 11 new comedy projects as part of their development slate for 2018, including a new scripted program from Oliver Platt.\u201cWe are privileged once again to be working with a variety of incredible talent on projects with unique comedic points of view, as well as creating more linear content from our digital platform, the Comedy Crib,\u201d said Jennifer Caserta, president of IFC, in a statement. \u201cThese projects come from gifted creative teams and are great examples of the distinct comedic storytelling that embodies IFC\u2019s trademark \u2018slightly off\u2019 sensibility.\u201d\u201cNGO\u201d is a half-hour executive produced by Platt and John Lyons and written and executive produced by Collin Friesen that follows a \u201cgin-soaked but charismatic head of a private West African charity\u00a0that he guides with a fist full of American cash in one hand and a rocket-launcher in the other.\u201dRelated Andie MacDowell Drama \u2018Love After Love\u2019 Lands at IFCJoining \u201cNGO\u201d on the roster today, alongside recently greenlit new series \u201cLiving With Yourself,\u201d which is set to premiere in 2018 and the previously announced \u201cHow To Rig An Election\u201d based on the book of the same name by former political consultant Allen Raymond, is \u201cSherman\u2019s Showcase.\u201d A sketch comedy show inspired by \u201cSoul Train\u201d and from writer-performers Bashir Salahuddin and Diallo Riddle, \u201cSherman\u2019s Showcase\u201d will travel \u201cthrough time via music and comedy drawn from the forty-year library of a legendary, but fictional, musical variety show.\u201dAdditionally, IFC is developing \u201cElna,\u201d a half-hour scripted comedy about what it\u2019s like to be Mormon and lose your faith. Coming from Elna Baker, who writes, executive produces, and stars in the project, \u201cElna\u201d is also written and executive produced by Eric Gilliland and executive produced by Left Right Productions.\u201cGreener Grass\u201d is based on a short film of the same name, and comes from Upright Citizens Brigade alumni Jocelyn DeBoer and Dawn Luebbe as writers, executive producers, and stars. \u201cGreener Grass\u201d centers on a small town that looks like a living Norman Rockwell painting (and has a welcome sign that boasts \u201cNothing bad ever happens here\u201d) but hides a \u201cworld of bad things\u201d underneath the facade.\u201cThe Last Hotel Detective\u201d from writer and executive producer Gabe Delahaye centers on an in-house\u00a0private detective who is employed by the \u201chistoric and luxurious Palace Hotel to solve guests\u2019 minor crimes and delicate mysteries.\u201d IFC boasts him as \u201ca character out of a Raymond Chandler novel living in a digital world and the last of his kind.\u201dBased on the non-fiction book of the same name, \u201cDr. Taco\u201d comes from writers and executive producers Mike Barker, Carlo Hart, Evan Sandman and Keshet Studios. It\u2019s an ensemble piece about \u201csix American misfits\u201d who are unable to get into medical school in the U.S. and therefore find themselves \u201cgetting a very hands-on\u201d education in a cartel-controlled part of Mexico.Jay Kogen writes and executive produces \u201cBad Beta,\u201d about a \u201cbrilliant female scientist with relationship issues\u201d who is forced to care for a botched version of an A.I. she created.\u201cNew Hope\u201d focuses on New Hope University, a well-known evangelical Christian college in the Midwest run by \u201ca world famous but aging and increasingly senile religious leader,\u201d where a deep power struggle over control of the church and school abounds. \u201cNew Hope\u201d is written and executive produced by Boyd Hale.Based on the IFC Comedy Crib series, \u201cFrank and Lamar\u201d features two best friends who live together and work together as middle school teachers in New York City and have to deal with a wide variety of workplace drama, romantic entanglements, and friendship struggles. \u201cFrank and Lamar\u201d is created by Carl Foreman Jr. and Anthony Gaskins, who also star as the title characters.Josh Ruben and Vince Peone have created, written, and directed \u201cLee\u2019s Kill List,\u201d about a woman who takes her life into her own hands by killing the \u201cbullies and bosses\u201d that threaten her sales job.Written by Emily Wilson and animated by Emily Flake, \u201cMama Tried\u201d is based on Flake\u2019s graphic novel about the underbelly of new motherhood. The show follows the struggles of one woman who cares more about surviving motherhood than thriving as a \u201csuper-mom.\u201d The show is executive produced by Jax Media.Over on the digital side, IFC is also touting a few new Comedy Crib series, including \u201cJanice and Jeffrey\u201d from Matteo Lane and Mollie Merkel, \u201cCommuters\u201d from\u00a0Jared Warner, Nick Ciavarella and Tim Dean, and \u201cNeurotica\u201d from\u00a0Jenny Jaffe.\u201cJanice and Jeffrey\u201d follows its out comedians and creators (Lane and Merkel) who portray the closeted titular couple who meet in a Christian recovery program and get married the same week. As their marriage hits a rough patch, they go to extreme lengths to solve intimacy issues. \u201cJanice and Jeffrey\u201d is produced by Mustache.\u201cCommuters\u201d follows a couple who move to the suburbs and adapt to \u201call the joys that come\u201d with not trying to be hip in a city that never sleeps. \u201cCommuters\u201d is directed by Adam Wirtz and produced by Post Everything Productions.On \u201cNeurotica,\u201d which premieres August 21 on IFC.com, a dominatrix struggles with OCD and \u201cfights to maintain her business when a fancy new MegaDungeon opens in her small town.\u201d \u201cNeurotica\u201d is produced and directed by Jetpacks Go!.Filed Under:IFCOliver PlattTCA Want to read more articles like this one?SUBSCRIBE TO VARIETY TODAY.Post A Comment1 TV\u2018Mad Men\u2019 Stars Join Matthew Weiner Amazon SeriesTV\u2018Thrones\u2019 New Episode Leaks, Not Connected to HBO HackTV\u2018Karate Kid\u2019 Sequel Series Heads to YouTube RedTV\u2018Grey\u2019s Anatomy\u2019 Recasts Owen\u2019s Sister for Season 14Oscars: Venice, Telluride, Toronto Lineups Provide Awards Season Snapshot\u2018Detroit\u2019: Annapurna\u2019s Pulse-Pounding Debut Release Aims for Oscar GoldChristopher Nolan\u2019s \u2018Dunkirk\u2019 Is the First Slam-Dunk Oscar Contender of 2017\u2018The Handmaid\u2019s Tale\u2019 Scores Hulu Its First Big Emmy NominationsVariety JobsCreative Development CoordinatorTriptyk Studios, New York, New YorkAccount/Project Coordinator (Ad Agency)Petrol Advertising, Burbank, CaliforniaMedia SpecialistBridgewater Associates, Westport, ConnecticutExecutive Director, Aspen FilmCompany Confidential, Aspen, ColoradoAssociate Director, YouTuber & Gamer PartnershipsSave the Children, Fairfield, ConnecticutSee More Jobs MarketplaceLeave a Reply 1 Comment Cancel reply Comments are moderated.
