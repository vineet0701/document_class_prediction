Navigation MenuFilm TV Digital Music Contenders Video Dirt SceneMoreBiz Legit Jobs Conferences Insight Archives Directory Choose Edition:U.SGlobalAsiaRussia|My AccountSign InSubscribe HomeTVNews WGN America Nabs Murder Mystery Series \u2018Bellevue\u2019 Starring Anna PaquinElizabeth WagmeisterTV Reporter@EWagmeister Share This ArticleLinkedInEmailPrintTalkJan Thijs/WGN AmericaJuly 31, 2017 | 12:00PM PTWGN America has acquired the U.S. rights to new drama series \u201cBellevue,\u201d starring Anna Paquin,\u00a0Variety has learned.Set to premiere on WGN America in early 2018, \u201cBellevue\u201d hails from Muse Entertainment and Back Alley Films. Paquin stars alongside Allen Leech and Shawn Doyle, and will also serve as an executive producer on the series.Bellevue is a small town with big secrets where 20 years ago, the murder of a young woman traumatized the community. Now the killer is back \u2014 or is he? In the series, when a high school hockey star wrestling with his gender identity goes missing and all signs point to foul play, Detective Annie Ryder (Paquin) must unravel all the pieces to this gripping mystery before her own life falls apart. As the case pulls her further away from her family, she is also confronted by a mysterious person from her past with disturbing answers and a terrifying need to get inside her head. Along the way, Annie must navigate a complicated relationship with her on again, off again ex, Eddie (Leech) and her boss on the force, Police Chief Peter Welland (Doyle).Related Anna Paquin to Star in Netflix Miniseries \u2018Alias Grace\u2019\u201cBellevue\u201d was created by Jane Maggs and Adrienne Mitchell, who will serve as co-showrunners and executive producers, alongside Paquin. Mitchell is the lead director on the series, and\u00a0April Mullen\u00a0and\u00a0Kim Nguyen will also direct other episodes.\u00a0The season will span eight episodes, premiering in the U.S. after the Canadian debut on CBC.Academy Award winner Paquin is best known for starring in HBO\u2019s \u201cTrue Blood\u201d and the \u201cX-Men\u201d franchise. Up next, she will star in Netflix\u2019s upcoming Margaret Atwood adaptation \u201cAlias Grace.\u201d\u00a0She is repped by WME.For WGN America, acquiring \u201cBellevue\u201d comes at a time when the network is shifting away from producing original content, so the Muse and Back Alley produced project fits into the cabler\u2019s new strategy.
